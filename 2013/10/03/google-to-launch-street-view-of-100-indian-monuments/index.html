GOOGLE TO LAUNCH STREET VIEW OF 100 INDIAN MONUMENTS

by bensonshaji . 03 Oct 2013    
Tweet

Finally Google has made a tie up with Archaeological Society of India (ASI) to launch street view of Indian monuments. Google has already started collecting data of some of the top 100 monuments in India such as Taj Mahal, Qutub Minar etc.. Once the data has been collected and processed, it will be launched online for web users to explore Indian monuments with a full 360 degree view.

Google is using a special tool called street view trekker to capture the pictures. This is a wearable backpack with a camera mounted on top of it, this camera is used to gather 360 degree imagery while maneuvering through tight, narrow spaces or locations only accessible by foot. This technology was first experimented and implemented along the rough, rocky, terrain of Arizona's Grand Canyon.

Google said that it would take 7-10 days to capture a complete 360 degree imagery of individual monument. So we can assume that it might take up to 2-3 years in order to complete capturing 100 Indian monuments. Mean while Google is trying to get full permission to capture street view of Indian roads in the next two years.
