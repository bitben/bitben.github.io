COMET ISON HEADING FOR CLOSE ENCOUNTER WITH SUN

by bensonshaji . 13 Nov 2013

A comet named ISON is heading towards sun for a close encounter, the comet is supposed to vapourize at that temperature but the chances are that it won't... says the researchers. Comet ISON is travelling at a great speed of 377 km/sec and is expected to reach 1 million kilometers near to the sun's surface by the end of november i,e on 28. Scientist are not sure how ISON will hold up such a heat of about 2760 degrees, which is hot enough to vapourize not only ice, but also metals and rocks. Even if that temperature does not vapourize the comet, the gravitational force of the sun would rip it apart. But the recent calculations showed that ISON will survive in both the cases. If the predictions and calculations are proved correct, then the comet could be visible to the naked eye in early december and throughout the night begining in January.
