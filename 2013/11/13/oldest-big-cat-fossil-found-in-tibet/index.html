OLDEST BIG CAT FOSSIL FOUND IN TIBET

by bensonshaji . 13 Nov 2013    
Tweet

The oldest big cat fossils have been unearthed in Himalayas, these unknown species are similar to snow leopard, lived 4.1 to 5.95 million years ago and has been named Panthera. This discovery in Tibet supports the theory that big cats evolved in Central Asia and not Africa.

Scientist used both anatomical and DNA data to determine that the skulls belonged to an extinct big cat, whose territory appears to overlap many of the species we know today.

This cat is a sister of living snow leopards as the fossil found has a broad forehead and a short face. But it's little smaller - the size of clouded leopards.
