PLATYPUS GODZILLA FOSSIL UNEARTHED IN AUSTRALIA

by bensonshaji . 05 Nov 2013    
Tweet

Part of giant platypus fossil has been unearthed in Queensland, Australia. Scientist have dubbed it as Platypus-zilla because of its huge size which is more than 1 meter in length. The researchers also said that the creature lived between five and 15 million years ago. This discovery of the giant platypus indicates there are branches in the platypus family tree that we did not suspect before.

The discovery of the giant platypus is a single fossil-ed tooth, which was unearthed in the River sleigh fossil beds in northwest Queensland. Based on its size the scientist could calculate overall size, and bumps on its teeth and other fossil finds nearby suggest that crustaceans, turtles, frogs and fish were its feast.

The discovery of this platypus-zilla was a bit of shock to the scientist, as it made them to realize that the platypus's story they knew about it, is increasingly more complicated than their thoughts. The researchers have a hope that they could find more platypus fossils in the same region that can lead to study more about these creatures.
