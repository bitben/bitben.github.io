RARE SOLAR ECLIPSE IN AMERICA, EUROPE AND AFRICA

by bensonshaji . 03 Nov 2013

A rare solar eclipse that is said to be the "Hybrid" of annular and total eclipse is visible in some parts of America, Europe and Africa. This kind of eclipse allows a view of sun that is totally or partially blocked out by Moon. Southern United states found to be the first place where solar eclipse was visible and is moving towards east across African continent. The total eclipse will be further seen towards east across the African continent and partial eclipse will be seen in eastern North America and Southern Europe. Experts warn not to view the eclipse with naked eyes.

In a total eclipse, the moon completely covers the sun, while an annular eclipse occurs when the moon is at its farthest from the earth and does not block out the sun completely, leaving a halo sunlight still visible around the moon. NASA has announced that the best visibility of the total eclipse was seen from Gabon.
