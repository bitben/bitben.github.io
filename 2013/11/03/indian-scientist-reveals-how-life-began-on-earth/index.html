INDIAN SCIENTIST REVEALS HOW LIFE BEGAN ON EARTH

by bensonshaji . 03 Nov 2013

Indian-origin palentologist, Dr. Sankar Chatterjee belives that the impact of icy comets that colloided and melted with the organic compounds that were deposited by meteorites over 3.8 billion years ago might have paved a way to the formation of molecules and eventually life.

He belives that meteorites containing organic materials on them might have crashed earth followed by the icy comet impact made them to be filled by water. Where repeative meteorites crash made volcanically driven geothermal vents in the earth's crust that gets heated up and stirred the water naturally. This chemical mixture led to the formation of molecules of ever increasing complexity - and eventually life. He could possibly come to this conclusion based on his studies on the sites that contained world's oldest fossil in Greenland, Australia, and South Africa.
