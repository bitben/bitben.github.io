SUPERNOVA TO BE SEEN FROM EARTH BY 2063

by bensonshaji . 06 Nov 2013    
Tweet

Astronomers have calculated that in the next 50 years, a supernova is occurring in our home galaxy that can be visible from earth and the chances are 100%. It will be visible from telescopes in the form of infrared radiation. The chances are 20% or less so that this event can be visible or identified through naked eye as shining stellar spectacle in the night sky.

This event gives an opportunity to the astronomers and scientist to study something that's never been discoverd before. It can also help them to detect the very initial stage of star's demise.

What is actually supernova? A massive star goes supernova at the movement when it has utilized all its nuclear fuel and its core collapses, just before it explodes violently and throws off most of its mass into space. Many stars have gone supernova in the past but in other galaxies, it is the first time we could calculate that the supernova is going to take place in our Milky-way galaxy by 2063.
