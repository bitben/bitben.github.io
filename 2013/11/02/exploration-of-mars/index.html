EXPLORATION OF MARS TO BE LAUNCHED

by bensonshaji . 02 Nov 2013

For those who were hoping a lot more in mars other than rocks and methane, NASA has announced to launch its MAVEN(Mars Atmospheric and Volatile EvolutioN) probe to Mars this month to find out why red planet lost much of its atmosphere. Long ago, Mars once had denser atmosphere that supported liquid water on the surface, which might have had environmental conditions to support microbial life, based on long-term presence of water.

Most of the martian atmosphere might have gone lost to space as part of climate change long ago, resulting a thin martian atmosphere that no longer allows water to be stable at the surface. MAVEN will be the first spacecraft ever to make direct measurements of the upper martian atmosphere to find out when and for how long mars might have had an environment that could have supported microbial life in its ancient past.

MAVEN will have to travel for about 10 months to Mars inorder to start its yearlong orbiting mission.
