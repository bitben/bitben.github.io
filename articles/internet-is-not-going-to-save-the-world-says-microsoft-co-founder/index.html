Internet is not going to save the world says the microsoft co-founder

posted on: 04-11-2013 21:00 PM / written by: bensonshaji / For: technology, microsoft, internet, social-network

Popularity

Tweet

These days we can see that west coast billionaires Facebook and Google are much interested in making internet available to billions of people around the world, where as the Microsoft co-founder says the basic things like child survival, child nutrition or malarial vaccine is much important than internet.

These were the interesting statements made by Microsoft co-founder Bill Gates during an exclusive interview with FT. Even though Bill gates describes himself as a technocrat, he does not believe that technology will save the world. Or, to be more precise, he does not believe it can solve a tangle of entrenched and interrelated problems that afflict humanity’s most vulnerable: the spread of diseases in the developing world. He certainly love the IT thing but if we have to improve lives, then we got to deal with more basic things like child survival, child nutrition or probably a malarial vaccine.

West coast billionaires of Google and Facebook have a vision and mission of making internet available to the billions of unconnected people around the world. It is said to be “One of the greatest challenges of our generation”. But is it a priority to provide the planet with internet connection rather than a malarial vaccine. Gates being sarcastic echoed many times that “When some one is dying of malaria, Is he supposed to look up and seek help from that Google Internet Balloon?”

The reality everyone should understand is that the world is not flat and PCs or Internet are not in the hierarchy of human needs. There are many other basics things which requires priority.
